Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist Specifically for College Students in India

CSE0001 – Digital Literacy
March 2026

Account & Password Security 

•	Stop reusing passwords. Every account must have a unique password. Use Google Password Manager, Bitwarden, or any other free password manager - they remember everything for you.
•	Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your email, LinkedIn, GitHub, and banking apps. Seriously, it is the most effective single step you can take to secure your online identity Even if someone knows your password, they still cannot log in without your phone.
•	Keep your apps and phone operating system updated. Yes, it's not just new features - it's also new security fixes, which scammers exploit daily.

UPI & Financial Safety

•	Enter your UPI PIN to send money out, not to receive money in. If someone asks you to enter your UPI PIN to "receive" money, it's a scam! There are no exceptions to this!
•	Never scan a QR code sent by a buyer to "receive" money. The buyer must send money to your UPI handle, not the other way around. A buyer never has to scan a QR code to receive money! This is one of the most common OLX/Quikr scam types!
•	Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your bank's app. It won't completely stop scams, but it'll limit your losses per incident!
•	Always verify the name displayed on the UPI screen before you confirm any payment. Fake UPI IDs may appear legitimate but read carefully.


Phishing & Links 

•	Avoid clicking on links from unsolicited messages. This includes messages on WhatsApp, Instagram, or emails. If you receive a link from a person you don't know, don't click on it. If you receive a link from a person you know, don't click on it unless you're absolutely sure.
•	Be wary of URLs. Phishing websites may look identical to legitimate websites but may differ slightly. For example, instead of amazon.com, you may see arnazon.com.

Identity & Privacy

•	Avoid posting your Aadhaar, PAN, student ID, or bank documents anywhere. This includes private messages too. This information may be used to cause you serious identity-related problems.
•	Periodically review app permissions. Some apps may require permissions such as access to your camera, microphone, or location. Some apps may require permissions you don't need. Go to Settings > Apps > Permissions.
•	Periodically review your social media privacy settings. This includes Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, etc.

What to Do When You’re a Victim of Cybercrime

•	If you or any person you know has been a victim of cybercrime, you can report it on: National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: cybercrime.gov.in 
•	The earlier you report, the more likely you are to get a chargeback. "Being digitally safe isn't about being paranoid; it's about being aware of a few basic rules and following them.
